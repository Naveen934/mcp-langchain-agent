Tamil music has a rich and ancient history, deeply intertwined with the cultural and spiritual traditions of the Tamil people in South India and Sri Lanka. Its origins can be traced back over 2,000 years, with references found in Sangam literature (circa 300 BCE–300 CE). Classical Tamil music evolved alongside Carnatic music, one of the two main subgenres of Indian classical music, and has distinct characteristics like the use of Tamil lyrics, folk traditions, and unique ragas.

The development of Tamil music saw significant influence from the Bhakti movement (6th–9th centuries CE), where poet-saints like the Nayanmars (Shaiva saints) and Alwars (Vaishnavite saints) composed devotional songs in Tamil, forming the backbone of Tamil liturgical music.
